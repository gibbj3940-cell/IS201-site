Analysis

What the records show

College football records often reflect both elite talent and offensive systems. Modern passing numbers have grown as offenses have become faster and more pass-heavy, while career records also depend on durability and playing time.

For example, Case Keenum built his passing record over a high-volume Houston offense, while Donnel Pumphrey combined long-term consistency with a major senior-season rushing total. Corey Davis and Jarett Dillard show how productive Group of Five stars can still own national records.
