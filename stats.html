Why college football records are interesting

Career records are more than big numbers. They usually combine talent, opportunity, offensive system, health, and the era a player competed in. A passing leader from a high-tempo offense tells a different story than a rushing leader from a run-heavy program.

How I compare players

  1. Start with the main stat category.
    • Passing yards for quarterbacks
    • Rushing yards for running backs
    • Receiving yards and touchdowns for receivers
  2. Consider context.
    • Era of college football
    • Offensive system
    • Strength of schedule
  3. Look for consistency across multiple seasons.
